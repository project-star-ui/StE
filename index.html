The Amazing Esophagus!

Your Food's Super Slide!

What is the Esophagus?

The esophagus is like a super slide for your food! It's a muscular tube that connects your mouth to your stomach.

When you swallow, the esophagus uses special muscles to push food down in a wave-like motion called peristalsis!

Length

The esophagus is about 10 inches long in adults - that's about as long as a ruler!

Muscles

It has special muscles that work automatically to push food down, even if you're upside down!

Speed

Food takes just 5-8 seconds to travel through the esophagus - faster than you can say "yum"!

How the Esophagus Works

  1. 1. You chew food in your mouth
  2. 2. You swallow, and the food enters the esophagus
  3. 3. Muscles squeeze in waves to push food down
  4. 4. Food arrives in your stomach for digestion!

Fun Facts!

Space Swallowing

Astronauts can swallow in space because the esophagus works even without gravity!

Upside Down Eating

You could eat upside down and food would still reach your stomach!

Water Slide

Liquids take just 1 second to travel through the esophagus!

Muscle Power

The esophagus has two layers of muscles that work together like a team!
